Typewriter Play

Here I document the finds and frustrations, cleaning and lubing, cringeing and admiring, ofsome of the most important machines of the last century and a half.

I have fond memories of smashing the keys to get the type bars stuck, pulling out the ribbon, dinging the bell by throwing the carriage return right, and trying to lift that heavy piece of green metal. But I'm not 4 anymore, and my mother assures me that this first typewriter experience only lasted a few days or so before she got sick of it and let my brother tear it apart for fun. It made such an impact that when I finally got my hands on one as an adult, my intentions to find a sweet display piece morphed into the need to make these old workhorses road worthy again.

I'll post my treasures as I find them, type up some specimens, log my repairs or failings, and let you know when one's up for sale. Maybe my wife will join the party... Every other Friday is a treasure hunt in Kansas City, and the weekend is gonna be Damn Fine!
